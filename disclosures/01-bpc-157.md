# BPC-157: Half-Life Extension via Non-Canonical Amino Acids

## 1. Background

### 1.1 Native Peptide

**Body Protection Compound 157 (BPC-157)** is a synthetic pentadecapeptide derived from a partial sequence of human gastric juice protein BPC. It has demonstrated cytoprotective and regenerative properties across multiple organ systems in preclinical studies, including acceleration of wound healing, tendon and ligament repair, gastrointestinal mucosal protection, and neuroprotective effects.

- **Native Sequence:** `Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val`
- **Single-Letter Code:** `GEPPPGKPADDAGLV`
- **Molecular Formula:** C₆₂H₉₈N₁₆O₂₂
- **Molecular Weight:** ~1,419 Da
- **Isoelectric Point:** ~4.2
- **Known Stability Issues:** Susceptible to endopeptidases at Gly-Glu, Gly-Lys, and Gly-Leu junctions; exopeptidases at both termini; the three glycine residues (positions 1, 6, 13) create highly flexible regions that are preferentially targeted by proteases.

### 1.2 Therapeutic Relevance

BPC-157 has shown efficacy in animal models for:
- Gastrointestinal healing (ulcer, fistula, inflammatory bowel disease models)
- Musculoskeletal repair (tendon, ligament, bone, muscle)
- Neuroprotection and neuroregeneration
- Vascular protection and angiogenesis
- Cytoprotection against NSAID and alcohol-induced damage

The peptide interacts with the nitric oxide (NO) system, growth factor signaling (VEGF, EGF), and the FAK-paxillin pathway. Its short half-life in vivo (estimated minutes to low single-digit hours depending on route of administration) limits its therapeutic utility and necessitates frequent dosing.

### 1.3 Problem Statement

The native BPC-157 sequence contains three glycine residues and multiple protease-susceptible junctions. In vivo, the peptide is rapidly degraded by serum proteases and cleared by renal filtration. Extending the half-life while preserving the pharmacophore would enable less frequent dosing and improved therapeutic outcomes.

## 2. Disclosed Modifications

### 2.1 Variant A — Conservative D-Amino Acid Substitution

**Modified Sequence:**
```
Ac-[D-Gly¹]-Glu²-Pro³-Pro⁴-Pro⁵-[D-Gly⁶]-Lys⁷-Pro⁸-Ala⁹-[D-Asp¹⁰]-Asp¹¹-Ala¹²-[D-Gly¹³]-Leu¹⁴-Val¹⁵-NH₂
```

**Modifications:**
| Position | Native | Modified | Rationale |
|----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| N-terminus | Free amine | Acetyl cap (Ac) | Blocks aminopeptidase recognition; acetylation is metabolically stable |
| 1 | Gly | D-Gly | Resists L-specific aminopeptidases; Gly has no side chain so D/L switch has minimal steric impact on binding |
| 6 | Gly | D-Gly | Gly⁶ sits at a protease-susceptible junction (Pro⁵-Gly⁶-Lys⁷); D-substitution disrupts endopeptidase recognition without altering backbone trajectory (Gly is achiral, but D-configuration is defined by the Cα-H orientation in the peptide bond context) |
| 10 | Asp | D-Asp | The Ala⁹-Asp¹⁰ bond is an aspartyl protease target; D-Asp disrupts the substrate geometry |
| 13 | Gly | D-Gly | Protects the Ala¹²-Gly¹³-Leu¹⁴ stretch from metalloprotease cleavage |
| C-terminus | Free carboxyl | Amide (NH₂) | Blocks carboxypeptidase recognition |

**Expected Impact:** 5-10× increase in serum half-life. The Pro-Pro-Pro core (positions 3-5) is already protease-resistant; protecting the flanking Gly residues and termini addresses the primary degradation sites.

### 2.2 Variant B — Aib/PEG Hybrid

**Modified Sequence:**
```
Ac-[Aib¹]-Glu²-Pro³-Pro⁴-Pro⁵-[Aib⁶]-Lys⁷(PEG₂)-Pro⁸-[NMe-Ala⁹]-[D-Asp¹⁰]-Asp¹¹-Ala¹²-[Aib¹³]-Leu¹⁴-Val¹⁵-NH₂
```

**Modifications:**
| Position | Native | Modified | Rationale |
|----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| 1, 6, 13 | Gly | α-Aminoisobutyric acid (Aib) | Aib is the α,α-dimethyl analog of Gly; it enforces 3₁₀-helical conformation and provides steric shielding of the backbone amide bonds. The gem-dimethyl substitution prevents protease access while maintaining the backbone flexibility needed at these positions |
| 7 | Lys | Lys(PEG₂) | Conjugation of a mini-PEG₂ (8-amino-3,6-dioxaoctanoic acid) to the ε-amine of Lys⁷ increases the hydrodynamic radius, reducing renal filtration. The PEG moiety is attached via an amide bond to the side chain, preserving the backbone structure |
| 9 | Ala | N-methyl-Ala (NMe-Ala) | N-methylation of the backbone nitrogen eliminates the NH hydrogen bond donor, preventing protease binding at this position. NMe-Ala maintains the side chain methyl group of Ala |
| 10 | Asp | D-Asp | As in Variant A |

**Expected Impact:** 10-20× increase in serum half-life. The PEG₂ addition provides an additional mechanism of clearance reduction beyond protease resistance alone.

### 2.3 Variant C — β-Amino Acid Hybrid

**Modified Sequence:**
```
Ac-[β³-hGly¹]-Glu²-Pro³-Pro⁴-Pro⁵-[β³-hGly⁶]-Lys⁷-Pro⁸-Ala⁹-Asp¹⁰-Asp¹¹-Ala¹²-[β³-hGly¹³]-Leu¹⁴-Val¹⁵-NH₂
```

**Modifications:**
| Position | Native | Modified | Rationale |
|----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| 1, 6, 13 | Gly | β³-homoGlycine (β-Ala) | Replacement of α-Gly with β-Gly (β-alanine) inserts an additional methylene unit into the backbone at these positions. This creates an α/β-peptide hybrid that is not recognized by any known human protease. The additional backbone length at three positions will alter the overall peptide conformation, but the flexible nature of both Gly and β-Ala may preserve biological activity |

**Expected Impact:** 15-30× increase in serum half-life, but with higher risk of activity loss due to backbone length changes. This variant should be considered exploratory.

## 3. Synthesis Considerations

### 3.1 General Strategy

All variants are amenable to solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS) using standard Fmoc chemistry:

- **Resin:** Rink amide MBHA resin (for C-terminal amide)
- **Coupling reagents:** HATU/DIPEA or DIC/Oxyma for standard couplings; extended coupling times (2×45 min) for positions following NMe-amino acids or Aib (sterically hindered)
- **D-amino acids:** Commercially available as Fmoc-D-Xaa-OH; no special handling required
- **Aib:** Fmoc-Aib-OH requires double coupling with HATU due to steric hindrance of the gem-dimethyl group
- **NMe-Ala:** Fmoc-NMe-Ala-OH; coupling to the subsequent residue may require microwave-assisted SPPS or use of acid chloride activation
- **PEG₂ conjugation (Variant B):** Fmoc-NH-PEG₂-COOH coupled on-resin to Lys⁷ side chain after selective Dde/ivDde deprotection

### 3.2 Purification

Reverse-phase HPLC (C18 column, water/acetonitrile gradient with 0.1% TFA) should achieve >95% purity. Expected elution order: Variant C (most polar) < Variant A < Variant B (most lipophilic due to PEG and NMe).

### 3.3 Quality Control

- Identity: ESI-MS or MALDI-TOF
- Purity: Analytical RP-HPLC (>95%)
- Sequence confirmation: MS/MS fragmentation
- Copper-free (for this peptide — not applicable, but standard QC for peptide APIs)
